📅 SIMPLIFIED COMMUNICATION TIMELINE

Time Window What Happens
0-10 min Get everyone on bridge • Page resolver teams • Open ServiceNow ticket • Turn on transcription • Pre-stage templates
10-30 min Complete initial assessment • Confirm scope and impact • Decide path (A-E) • If Path A: Send IS Notifier All-Staff ALERT
>30 min Continue comms based on path • Path C, B, D, or E actions • Begin update cadence (Critical: 30min, High: 60min, Medium: 2-3hrs)
End Send all clear communications • Official all clear for paths A-D • Path E: no communication needed

⬆️ ESCALATION & PATH TRANSITIONS

  • Path E → D/B/A: Issue persists >30 min, scope expands, users start calling, patient safety emerges
  • Path D → B/A: Multiple departments affected, exceeds 2 hours, patient safety concerns
  • Path B → A: Impact broader than expected, immediate all-staff action needed
  • Path A → C: All-staff alerted, now AOC needs additional context and dedicated channel

⏰ UPDATE CADENCE BY INCIDENT SEVERITY LEVEL

🔴 CRITICAL IMPACT (P1)

  • Systems: Patient Safety/EHR/Network/Security
  • Frequency: Every 30 minutes
  • Audience: All-staff + AOC (Path A → Path C)

🟡 HIGH IMPACT

  • Systems: Significant Workflow Disruption
  • Frequency: Every 60 minutes
  • Audience: AOC + departments (Path B sequence)

🟢 MEDIUM IMPACT

  • Systems: Limited Impact
  • Frequency: Every 2-3 hours
  • Audience: AOC only (Path D)

📋 MESSAGE TEMPLATE REQUIREMENTS

🚨 ALERT Messages:

  • What system/service is affected
  • Impacted Users or Location
  • What to Know (impact on workflows)
  • What is Next (next communication time)

🔄 UPDATE Messages:

  • What system/service is affected
  • Impacted Users or Location
  • Update (progress since last communication)
  • What to Do (new workarounds)
  • What is Next (next update time)

✅ ALL CLEAR Messages:

  • What system/service is affected
  • Impacted Users or Location
  • What to Know (service restored)
  • What to Do (precautions/contacts)
🔄 CONTINUOUS REASSESSMENT
Key Decision Questions: Has scope/impact changed? • Do I have new information? • Taking longer than expected? • Patient safety considerations changed?
Review Frequency: Every 30-60 minutes during active incident
